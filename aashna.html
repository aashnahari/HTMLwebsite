Shih Tzu!

by Aashna



Overview:

The Shih Tzu (UK: /ˌʃiːˈtsuː/, US: /ˈʃiːtsuː/; Chinese: 西施犬; pinyin: Xī Shī quǎn literally "Hsi Shih dog") is a toy dog breed originating from Tibet and having been bred from the Pekingese and the Lhasa Apso. Shih Tzus are well known for their short snout and large round eyes, as well as their ever growing coat, floppy ears, and short and stout posture. Although small in size, they are well known for their largely fun and playful personality, and calm and friendly temperament. They are able to adapt well in different situations. Due to their highly independent nature, they are not considered the most obedient breed.

Characteristics:
  1. sturdy little dog
  2. small muzzle
  3. large dark brown eyes
  4. a soft and long double coat that will tangle
  5. stand no more than 26.7 cm (101⁄2")
  6. an ideal weight of 4.0 to 7.5 kg (9 to 17 lb)


Fun facts:
  • name comes from the Chinese language word for "lion"
  • nicknamed the Chrysanthemum Dog in England in the 1930s
  • favorites of the Chinese royals
  • fourteen total iterations of the Shih Tzu in the breed's gene pool today
  • the Shih Tzu ranked the 15th most popular breed in 2013
